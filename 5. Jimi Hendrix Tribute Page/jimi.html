Jimi Hendrix

November 27, 1942 - September 18, 1970

Here's a time line of Jimi Hendrix's life:

  • November 27, 1942 - James Marshall Hendrix born in Seattle, Washington
  • September 24, 1966 - Brought to London, UK
  • October 13, 1966 - The Jimi Hendrix Experience performed their first show at the Novelty in Evreux
  • October 23, 1966 - recorded "Hey Joe"
  • November 2, 1966 - recorded "Stone Free", Hendrix's first written song
  • December 16, 1966 - released first single (A - "Hey Joe", B - "Stone Free")
  • December 29, 1966 - "Hey Joe" entered UK charts and peaked at number 6
  • March 1967 - "Purple Haze" hit number three UK charts
  • May 1967 - "The Wind Cries Mary" peaked at number six on UK charts and remained on the charts for eleven weeks
  • May 12, 1967 - the album "Are You Experienced" was released in the UK, peaking at number two
  • May 1, 1967 - "Hey Joe" released in America
  • June 18, 1967 - The Jimi Hendrix Experience play their first US concert at the Monterey Pop Festival
  • December 1, 1967 - "Axis: Bold as Love" released
  • October 25, 1968 - "Electric Ladyland" released
  • June 29, 1969 - the last performance of the original Experience lineup
  • August 18, 1969 - Jimi Hendrix headlines Woodstock
  • September 18, 1970 - dies of asphyxia while intoxicated with barbiturates at the age of 27.

"When the power of love overcomes the love of power the world will know peace."

- Jimi Hendrix
